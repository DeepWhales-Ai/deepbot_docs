<H2 id="frame">The frame</H2>
<Para>
  Most crypto signals are scraped from the outside. A bot watches a chain, a feed parses a
  social timeline, a scanner reads a contract. Each is one perspective on the market, and each
  is one easy thing to game.
</Para>
<Para>
  Bobby is the opposite. Bobby is already inside the rooms where coordination happens. The
  network's view of the market is the view from inside every project community at
  once.
</Para>

<H2 id="why">Why it matters</H2>
<Pull>
  Convergence is the thing single source tools cannot do.
</Pull>
<Para>
  A push that fires across many independent communities at the same time looks different from
  a push that fires inside one. The first is a market event. The second is a single group of
  people. Bobby distinguishes the two by default. Single source tools cannot.
</Para>
