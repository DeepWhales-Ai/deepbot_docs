<PageHead
  eyebrow="DALE"
  lede="Two ways to protect a position. Hard stop loss locks the floor from entry. Trailing stop loss locks gains from the peak."
/>

<H2 id="hard">Hard stop loss</H2>
<Para>
  Fixed loss percentage from your entry price. Triggers once the token drops
  that percentage below your entry. Sells the position.
</Para>
<Para>
  <Mono>Entry $1.00, hard stop loss 30%.</Mono><br />
  <Mono>Threshold: $0.70.</Mono><br />
  <Mono>Price drops to $0.70 or below → sells.</Mono>
</Para>
<Para>
  The floor is fixed. It does not move with price.
</Para>

<H2 id="trailing">Trailing stop loss</H2>
<Para>
  Loss percentage from the highest price the token has reached since you
  entered. The peak updates as the token climbs. The trigger threshold rides
  up with it.
</Para>
<Para>
  This protects gains automatically without you having to keep moving the
  stop.
</Para>

<H3>Scenario: price rises then crashes</H3>
<Para>
  50% trailing stop loss. Entry $1.00.
</Para>

| Tick | Price | Peak | Threshold (peak × 0.5) | Trigger? |
|------|-------|------|------------------------|----------|
| 1 | $1.00 | $1.00 | $0.50 | No |
| 2 | $1.50 | $1.50 | $0.75 | No |
| 3 | $2.00 | $2.00 | $1.00 | No |
| 4 | $1.80 | $2.00 | $1.00 | No |
| 5 | $1.00 | $2.00 | $1.00 | Sell |

<Para>
  Tick 5 fires because the peak was $2.00 and price has dropped 50% from that
  peak. Even though $1.00 is your original entry price, the trigger is
  calculated against the peak, not the entry.
</Para>

<H3>Scenario: dip and recovery</H3>
<Para>
  50% trailing stop loss. Entry $1.00.
</Para>

| Tick | Price | Peak | Threshold | Trigger? |
|------|-------|------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | $1.00 | $1.00 | $0.50 | No |
| 2 | $3.00 | $3.00 | $1.50 | No |
| 3 | $1.60 | $3.00 | $1.50 | No |
| 4 | $1.50 | $3.00 | $1.50 | Sell |

<Para>
  Price tripled, then fell back to $1.50, exactly the 50% trailing threshold
  off the $3.00 peak. <Lime>The trailing stop locks in the gain.</Lime> You
  exit at $1.50 against an entry of $1.00, even though the peak was $3.00.
</Para>

<H2 id="confirmation">Confirmation behaviour</H2>
<Para>
  Trailing stops use a <Lime>three tick confirmation</Lime> before firing.
  Price has to stay at or below the threshold for three consecutive checks.
  Wick recoveries reset the count.
</Para>
<Para>
  This protects against a single bad print triggering an unnecessary sell.
</Para>

<H2 id="how">How to use them</H2>
<Para>
  Hard stop loss works for any position where you have a clear "this trade is
  invalidated below X" line.
</Para>
<Para>
  Trailing stop loss works for trending positions where you want to ride the
  move but lock gains automatically as price extends.
</Para>
<Para>
  Both attach to Shotgun buys. Both can be set on manual buys through the{' '}
  <Mono>/trade</Mono> panel. Dale conversational setup is part of the
  standard exit toolkit.
</Para>
