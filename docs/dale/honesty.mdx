<H2 id="sent">What "sent" means</H2>
<Para>
  Dale only reports <Lime>Sent</Lime> or <Lime>Done</Lime> on actual on chain
  success. If a tool returns an error, Dale tells you the actual reason the
  tool returned. No invented diagnoses. No vague apologies. No claiming
  success when nothing happened.
</Para>

<ChatBlock>
  <ChatBubble from="user">buy 0.1 eth of pepe</ChatBubble>
  <ChatBubble from="dale">Tried. Router rejected: insufficient liquidity at your slippage. Raise slippage or try later.</ChatBubble>
</ChatBlock>

<H2 id="unknown">What "I don't know" means</H2>
<Para>
  When tools return no data, Dale says so. Dale does not guess at why. Dale
  does not fill silence with plausible sounding explanations.
</Para>

<ChatBlock>
  <ChatBubble from="user">what's the dev wallet on this token</ChatBubble>
  <ChatBubble from="dale">Scanner returned no deployer record for this contract. I do not have it.</ChatBubble>
</ChatBlock>

<H2 id="formatting">Outgoing message formatting</H2>
<Para>
  Dale's messages run through a formatting filter on the way out. Em dashes
  are stripped. Voice consistency holds. The product sounds like one product
  whether you are talking trade execution or wallet management.
</Para>

<H2 id="why">Why this matters</H2>
<Para>
  A trading partner that invents reasons or papers over failures is the
  most expensive thing you can have at the desk. Dale reports the tool's
  output. The tool decides what happened. <Lime>You decide what to do
  next.</Lime>
</Para>
