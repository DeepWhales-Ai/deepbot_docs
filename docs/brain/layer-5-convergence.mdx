<H2 id="why">A single ping is not an alert</H2>
<Para>
  Most crypto signal products work the same way. One source pings, an alert fires. Spam.
  Manipulation. Noise. The signal to noise ratio is bad on purpose because volume is what
  gets monetized.
</Para>
<Para>
  Convergence works differently. A single ping does not become an alert.{' '}
  <Lime>Convergence does.</Lime> When intelligence from multiple independent
  sources, weighted for adversarial robustness, agrees that something is happening, and only
  then, an alert fires.
</Para>

<H2 id="volume">Volume is excluded by design</H2>
<Para>
  Volume is the most manipulable input in crypto. Wash trading, paid pumps, automated
  rotation. The convergence engine does not weight volume into its scoring. Behavior across
  independent layers is what matters. Theatre is what the engine ignores.
</Para>

<H2 id="adversarial">Adversarial robust by construction</H2>
<Para>
  Manipulators can flood a single channel. They cannot flood every channel at once without
  leaving a footprint. The footprint is what the engine looks for. The harder a coordinated
  push tries to look organic, the more visible it becomes to convergence.
</Para>
