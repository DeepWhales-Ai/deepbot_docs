<H2 id="what">What this layer does</H2>
<Para>
  Every on-chain buy goes through a router. Routers have fingerprints. Gas patterns, slippage
  defaults, MEV behavior, calldata signatures. We map those fingerprints to behavioral tiers
  so the brain can answer the only question that matters: <Lime>is this signal real
  money or theatre?</Lime>
</Para>

<H2 id="why-fingerprints">Why fingerprints, not addresses</H2>
<Para>
  Wallet based smart money tracking is gameable. Anyone can spin up a fresh wallet. Routers
  are infrastructure. They cost real engineering and capital to operate, and the way each
  one signs and routes transactions is functionally a signature. Fingerprints are
  adversarially expensive to fake.
</Para>

<H2 id="convergence-input">What this feeds into</H2>
<Para>
  Router tier signal is one of the inputs into the convergence engine. A buy from a top-tier
  aggregator router is weighted differently from a retail-router buy of the same size. The
  engine never reveals the weighting publicly. <Mono>Twelve herbs and spices.</Mono>
</Para>

<Callout kind="lime" title="The investor read">
  A competitor would need to fingerprint every router on every chain, in real time, with
  adversarial-grade tier classification. None do. The work is the moat.
</Callout>
