<H2 id="dual-ws">Why two websockets</H2>
<Para>
  One websocket is a single point of failure. Two independent websockets, on independent
  infrastructure, give us redundancy at the latency layer. If the primary lags, the
  secondary fires. The cost is paying for two. The benefit is a system that does not blink
  when the primary node operator does.
</Para>
